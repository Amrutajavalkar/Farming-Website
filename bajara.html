Bajra Farming and Agriculture

• Growing Conditions:

Bajra thrives in hot, dry climates and is grown in regions with little rainfall. It is a hardy crop and requires minimal water, making it a valuable crop in areas suffering from water scarcity.

• Soil Requirements:

Bajra grows well in well-drained, loamy soils but can tolerate a variety of soil types.

• Planting and Harvesting:

Bajra is typically grown from seeds and harvested within 2-3 months after planting. It is often grown during the summer and rainy seasons.

• Pests and Diseases:

Bajra is relatively resistant to pests and diseases, but care must be taken against specific issues like stem borers, downy mildew, and leaf blight.
