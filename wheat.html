Wheat Farming and Agriculture

• Growing Conditions:

Wheat grows best in temperate climates, with well-drained soils. It requires moderate rainfall, but some varieties are more drought-tolerant.

• Soil Requirements:

Wheat prefers fertile, loamy soils with a pH level between 6.0 and 7.0.

• Planting and Harvesting:

Wheat is usually planted in the fall (for winter wheat) or spring (for spring wheat). The grain is harvested when the kernels are hard and the plants turn golden brown.

• Pests and Diseases:

Common wheat diseases include rust, blight, and fusarium head blight, which can be managed with crop rotation, resistant varieties, and good farming practices.
