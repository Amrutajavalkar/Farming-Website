Onion Farming and Agriculture

• Growing Conditions:

Onions grow best in well-drained, fertile soils with a pH between 6.0 and 7.5. They thrive in areas with moderate temperatures and plenty of sunlight.

• Watering Needs:

Onions require consistent moisture, especially during the initial growing stages, but can tolerate dry conditions once mature.

• Planting and Harvesting:

Onions are typically planted in early spring and harvested when the bulbs are mature and the tops begin to die off.

• Pests and Diseases:

Common pests include onion maggots, thrips, and aphids. Diseases like downy mildew, neck rot, and white rot can affect crops, and proper crop rotation and pest management practices are essential.
