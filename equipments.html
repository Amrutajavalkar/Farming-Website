1.Tractor

Plowing and Tilling: Tractors are equipped with plows and tillers to prepare soil for planting.
Transporting Loads: They can haul heavy loads such as bales of hay, tools, and equipment.
Mowing and Cutting: Tractors are often fitted with mower decks to cut grass or crops.
Planting and Harvesting: They are used with planting and harvesting attachments, including seed drills and harvesters.
Road and Construction Work: Tractors can be adapted with various implements to perform roadwork, digging, and material handling.

Popular tractor brands include:

  • John Deere
  • New Holland
  • Kuboto
  • Mahindra

2.Rotavator

Uses in Farm

Soil Aeration: By turning the soil, the rotary hoe allows air to reach plant roots more effectively, improving soil health and promoting root growth.
Fertilizer Incorporation: It can be used to mix fertilizers or compost into the soil, ensuring better distribution and absorption by plants.

Types of Rotary Hoes:

  • Self-propelled models: These are used for small to medium-sized fields, operated by a driver.

  • Tractor-mounted models: These are larger machines that are attached to a tractor and are used for bigger fields.

3. Center Pivot Irrigation Systems

  • Description: These are large, mechanized systems that rotate around a central point, typically powered by motors or pumps. They are widely used for large-scale farming.
  • Benefits: Efficient in water use, can cover large areas, and provide uniform distribution.
  • Applications: Suitable for crops like corn, wheat, and other field crops.

4. Drip Irrigation Systems

  • Description: This system delivers water directly to the root zone of plants via a network of tubes, emitters, or pipes. Though not technically a "sprinkler," it is often used for precise water delivery.
  • Benefits: Highly water-efficient, reduces water evaporation and runoff, minimizes weed growth.
  • Applications: Ideal for high-value crops like vegetables, orchards, and vineyards.
